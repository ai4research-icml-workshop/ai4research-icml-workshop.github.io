Call for Papers

Contribute Your Work

We welcome submissions that highlight successful workflows using AI for machine learning, math, and computer science research more generally. Contributions should illustrate, in a way that is accessible to non-experts, how a simple workflow has proven useful for cognitive research tasks by saving time, reducing effort, or strengthening results.

The workflow should be reproducible by ML researchers within a few hours and with academic-level financial resources. We therefore focus on simple strategies, or on more sophisticated strategies when submitters provide a package, agent, or repository that can be readily integrated through an API call.

Research Tasks and Challenges in Scope

  • AI-assisted research problem formulation
  • AI-assisted experiment design for ML research
  • Solving mathematical research problems with AI assistance
  • Formalization of mathematical research, especially as it is relevant for machine learning
  • Verification of AI-generated proofs
  • Automation of iterative loops
  • Other tasks that are integral to an AI-assisted research workflow

The focus is on how researchers can become more productive, more rigorous, and do better research with AI, and less on autonomous AI research, which is the focus of another exciting workshop: AI for Math 2026.

To maintain focus, the workshop does not emphasize tasks that are not purely research-centered, such as simple writing, basic literature search (“deep research”), slide and poster creation, or pure software engineering support such as package installation and version compatibility. There are already many resources available for such tasks.

What to Include in Your Submission

  • Explain the cognitive task in your research for which AI significantly saved time or improved the results you were able to obtain otherwise. Estimate the time savings or performance improvements.
  • Describe the workflow in which you used AI to help solve the task in a way that is reproducible and usable by other researchers within a few hours.
  • Include any relevant prompting techniques, code showing how you call the API, or a detailed description of the agentic framework you used.
  • If your workflow relies on web-based prompting, share the exact prompt and, ideally, the exact transcripts of the conversation.
  • If your workflow relies on an API-based interaction or agentic research setup, provide a link to a repository with your code.
  • Explain how you verified the correctness of the results.

Submission Format

Policies

  • Non-archival: papers published at other venues are welcome
  • Double-blind review: submissions must be anonymized
  • Accepted papers will be presented as demos or posters; select papers may be invited for contributed talks
  • Review criteria: accessibility, reproducibility, and correctness
