Call for Papers

Contribute Your Work

We welcome submissions that highlight successful workflows using AI for machine learning, math, and CS research. We are looking for contributions that demonstrate practical, reproducible methods — not just theoretical possibilities — for integrating AI into the research process.

Topics of Interest

  • AI-assisted mathematical research workflows: iterative verification loops, failure modes and how to detect them, prompting patterns that improve correctness, decomposition and self-critique, multi-agent strategies, and when to switch from informal to formal reasoning
  • Tool-augmented reasoning: integrating LLMs with computation (code, symbolic algebra, numerics), literature navigation, and proof assistants (e.g., Lean) to reduce hallucinations
  • Research acceleration in ML/CS: using AI for derivations, counterexample search, and experiment design, with an emphasis on methods that transfer across subfields

Submission Format

  • 4-page paper (workshop format), excluding references
  • Indicate preferred presentation format: computer demo or poster
  • Reviewed by organizers and/or external reviewers
  • Submissions via OpenReview
