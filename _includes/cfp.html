Call for Papers

Contribute Your Work

We welcome submissions that highlight successful workflows using AI for machine learning, mathematics, and computer science research. Submissions should explain, in a way that is accessible to non-experts, how a workflow helped with a meaningful cognitive research task by saving time, reducing effort, or strengthening results.

We are especially interested in workflows that ML researchers can reproduce within a few hours using typical academic resources. Simple strategies are welcome, and more sophisticated strategies are also in scope when authors provide a package, agent, or repository that can be integrated readily through an API call.

In Scope

  • AI-assisted research problem formulation
  • AI-assisted experiment design for ML research
  • Solving mathematical research problems with AI assistance
  • Formalization of mathematical research, especially as it is relevant for machine learning
  • Verification of AI-generated proofs
  • Automation of iterative loops
  • Other tasks that are integral to an AI-assisted research workflow

The emphasis is on helping researchers become more productive, more rigorous, and more effective with AI. The workshop is less about autonomous AI research, which is the focus of the related exciting AI for Math 2026 workshop.

Out of Scope

  • Simple writing support
  • Basic literature search (“deep research”)
  • Slide or poster creation
  • General software engineering support, such as package installation or version compatibility

What to Include in Your Submission

  • Explain the cognitive task in your research and estimate the time savings or performance improvements you obtained with AI assistance.
  • Describe the workflow clearly enough that another researcher could reproduce and use it within a few hours.
  • Include the key prompting techniques, API usage patterns, or agent setup needed to run the workflow.
  • If your workflow relies on web-based prompting, share the exact prompt and, ideally, the exact transcripts.
  • If your workflow relies on an API-based interaction or agentic setup, provide a link to a repository with your code.
  • Explain how you verified the correctness of the results.

Submission Format

  • 4-page paper (excluding references and supplementary materials)
  • Must follow {{ site.conference }} format
  • Indicate preferred presentation: computer demo or poster
  • Submissions via OpenReview

Policies

  • Non-archival: papers published at other venues are welcome
  • Double-blind review: submissions must be anonymized
  • Accepted papers will be presented as demos or posters; select papers may be invited for contributed talks
  • Review criteria: accessibility, reproducibility, and correctness
